“Constraints are a UML ‘Extensibility Mechanism’, that further defines Entities by expressing a value, condition or restriction that they must conform to, either individually and / or collectively; this is achieved by the use of textual statements or other metadata settings / values. Constraints may typically be applied to any Model Entity.”
There are no example figures here, they can however be found (where available) in the sections defining each usage.
Constraints are usually captured as metadata or within documentation for an entity and may (where possible) also be represented on diagrams, according to the methodology or modelling approach being employed and the type of constraint being considered.
The purpose of a constraint is to provide detail of any considerations that may affect how the content of / processing for a entity should be understood.
Constraints may be applied in the following modelling contexts:
Constraint context
Classes and their Attribute Constraints
Use Case Element Constraints (e.g. Triggers / Pre & Post Conditions / Goals)
Connector Constraints:
Activity Diagram Flow Constraints (Guard Conditions)
State Diagram Transition Constraints (Guard Conditions / Triggers / Effects)
Sequence Diagram Interaction Constraints (Timing Constraints)
The construction of UML Constraints are defined by the specific Core Notations for each application.
