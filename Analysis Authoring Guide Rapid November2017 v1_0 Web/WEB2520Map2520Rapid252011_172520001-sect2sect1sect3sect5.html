“A Sprint is a generally a time boxed period focussed on development of a product. Its purpose is to produce potentially shippable code, this is used to measure progress and aid planning.”
Who
Core Team.
Optional: Stakeholders.
Why (purpose)
The purpose of the Sprint Review meeting is to:
•  Demonstrates how the developed product meets the acceptance criteria (or not).
So that:
•  The Core Team gets feedback from the Product Owner so that they can help steer the development in the right direction.
•  The Product Owner is agreeing what has or hasn't been delivered.
When
At the end of each Sprint.
Duration
60 minutes.
What
A Sprint Review is a time to showcase the work of the team. They can be in a casual format or in a more formal meeting structure. The team should demonstrate work finished within the Sprint.
A Sprint Review is also a time to consider when, if and how to release the deliver a product increment.
The Business Analyst should update the work items status to implement from the Sprint in the analyst model.
New stories or detailed should be captured.
Any issues should be mediated by the Scrum Master.
Outputs
New or refined stories.
Agreement on list of work items and acceptance criteria delivered.
Agile Framework
Scrum and Kanban:
•  For Kanban, reviews should be aligned with team milestones rather than on a fixed cadence.
