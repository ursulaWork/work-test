Stories (‘Epic’ and ‘User’)
Introduction oNext: Story Mapping
Stories within Agile are descriptions of a feature/s told from the perspective of Stakeholder/s. Stories are described in the everyday or business language of Stakeholder/s.
There are 2 main types of Agile Stories: ‘Epic’, and ‘User’.
Epic Stories
‘Epic Stories’ are stories captured in high level business language from stakeholders describing stakeholder/s wants. Epics are large, coarse grained stories, often too big to implement in a single iteration or difficult to estimate against:
• 
Epic Stories will require breaking into smaller User Stories at some point,
• 
May not be formatted in the expected ‘As a <type of user>, I want <goal> so that I <receive benefit>’ format,
• 
May not include Acceptance Criteria,
• 
Epic Stories are held on the Backlog, but are usually low in priority until they are suitably refined into User Stories,
• 
Epic Stories can also be used as a planning tool in conjunction with a Story Map.
User Stories
User Stories are short, simple descriptions of a feature told from the perspective of the stakeholder.
The syntax of a User Story is:
‘As a [role name],
I want to [action],
So that [business goal].’
User Stories should be clear, feasible, and testable. All development team members should have a shared understanding of the story’s meaning; the story should not too big and comfortably fit into a sprint.
