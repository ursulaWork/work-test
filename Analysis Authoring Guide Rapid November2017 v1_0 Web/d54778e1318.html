The controlled copy of the Analysis Authoring Guide’s full content is maintained as a document by NHS Digital within its formal corporate document library (currently FileCM) under the File ID: NPFIT-SHR-MODL-GUID-0040.02.
Any copies of the Analysis Authoring Guide (or extracts from it) held outside of the NHS Digital corporate network, in whatever format (e.g. PDF, paper, web-output, e-mail attachment etc.,), are considered to have passed out of control and should always be checked for currency and validity. Publications or outputs that do not publish the full approved content for a given context (e.g. Readers Guides) MUST include the words ‘Extract’ as part of their main title.
NOTE: - IMPORTANT: Content published with a ‘Beta’ status is intended solely for internal use only, as a content development mechanism. It should NOT be distributed outside of the organisation as it may contain content that has not been verified and approved, or for which ownership permissions may not have been obtained.

The following guidelines may also assist users to use the Authoring Guide:
NOTE: - The content for this guide and the approved methodologies that underpin it are under constant development; readers should always ensure they have access to the most recent version when developing models utilising its guidance and instructions.
• 
The Analysis Authoring Guide’s structure is based around the organisation of the governing analysis methodology/ies.
• 
A contents listing is available at the front / beginning of the publication, detailing the aspects covered:
◦ 
A searchable alphabetical Index, as well as interactive content structure is provided for web versions.
• 
Page and Section numbering applied within a defined publication is unique to that generated output and may not follow that of other generated versions of this content.
• 
Hyperlinks for this publication are rendered as follows:
NOTE: - Where an output is an extract or subset of the full content, unresolved links for content that is not present are shown greyed out.

IMPORTANT! The rendering of related and navigational links places all of them at the beginning of a section or sub-section in a greyed out region. This approach is being implemented on an ad-hoc basis as time allows and therefore may not be present for all sections:
◦ 
The principles behind the ordering and presentation of links are as follows:
▪ 
First Level, either:
▫ 
Subsection/s: — links to subsections within the main topic (this line is omitted if there are none).
▫ 
‘Related content’ / ‘Additional related content’: — links to directly related content (e.g. Core Notations).
▪ 
Subsequent levels: — other indirectly related links or those placed for the users convenience (these may also be split into further levels for clarity (e.g. is a ‘kind-of’ or generalised links).
▪ 
Final Level (labelled: ‘Main Section/s’) — provides links to the parent section (if within a subsection), first page, or to any other relevant main section navigation pages (e.g. Modelling Concepts / Artefacts main navigation pages).
▪ 
All Web / HTML external links (e.g. website or intranet) are shown with the text in blue and underlined, in this format: Web page (External).
NOTE: - Some external web address URL’s may not be shown in full, to avoid rendering issues — these will be concatenated or replaced by a more meaningful label (right-clicking them reveals the full URL).
▪ 
Where a link is greyed-out, this indicates that the link does not resolve for this output (typically, that content is not included or the link is inoperable).
