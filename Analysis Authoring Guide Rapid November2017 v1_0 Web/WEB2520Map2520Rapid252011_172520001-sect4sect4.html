Heading Details
Product Name
Vision Statement
Description
“A Vision statement describes who the customers are, what customers need, and how these needs will be met. It captures the essence of the product – the critical information needed to develop and launch a product.”
Additional Description
(no content developed for this aspect at this time)
Purpose
The purpose of a Vision Statement is to bound and guide a project to avoid drift in requirements as well as to ensure that the target outcomes are kept in sight throughout the project’s life. As a result the Vision Statement should be revisited regularly throughout the lifecycle of a project.
Format & Content
The construct of a vision statement is: For <target customers> who <statement of the need or opportunity>, the <product name> is a <product category> that <key benefit or compelling reason to buy>. Unlike <primary competitive alternative, current system or current manual process>, our / this product <statement of primary product differentiation>.
A variation of this is the Problem Statement.
Construction Guidelines (Guidance)
The following provides additional understanding and guidance when developing a Vision Statement:
•  The template is designed to capture a Vision Statement for a project or system
•  The expression of a vision is a useful step in clarifying the intended outcome of any IT or business project
•  It should be created at the start of a project and agreed/validated with key stakeholders including the product owner, project manager and Senior Accountable Officer (SAO)
•  The Vision Statement will serve to bound and guide the project to avoid drift in requirements as well as to ensure that the target outcomes are kept in sight throughout the project’s life. As a result the vision should be revisited regularly throughout the lifecycle of the project
