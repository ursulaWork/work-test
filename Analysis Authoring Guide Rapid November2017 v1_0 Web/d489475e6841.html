Data Products projects consist of a series of Project Stages (or phases). Each stage has a prescribed set of activities that have to be undertaken, as well as products / artefacts available for development within the analysis model.
NOTE: - The specific stages of a project are determined by the methodology being utilised.

  Key stages for Rapid Methodology projects
Inception Stage
The business analyst will need to focus on the understanding the scope and objectives of the domain. The key deliverable produced by the business analyst will be the Analysis Scope & Objectives Definition document and agreement as to what methodology to use.
Discovery Stage
The Discovery stage builds on the scope agreed in the inception phase. The business analyst will be gaining a more detailed understanding of the domain. The Analysis model artefacts are built with provides a knowledge base for use by the project team. The analysis model will also support the planning and delivery of the product.
The project may produce a proposed release schedule for a product and a prototype during this stage.
Delivery Stage
The focus of the Delivery stage is the production of the product and the delivery will be broken down into a number of sprints for a release. The focus of the business analyst activities concerns supporting the team during the sprint and working on the artefacts for inclusion in future sprints. This means the analyst is working on at least two sprints concurrently.
Each Sprint is supported by a planning activities where the Analyst refines (or sometimes creates) the User Stories etc., identified as being within the scope of the corresponding Sprint. After planning the Analyst supports the artefacts during the Sprint, ensuring that they are completed / updated and match the sprint scope.
Closedown Stage
This may be the closure of the project or that the product has moved into a service delivery model where the business analyst is no longer required. The activities in this stage are to ensure a the business analysis artefacts can be agreed as complete or not in a controlled way.
Project Stage Controls
The focus of business analysis work activities changes between the stages as a product evolves. As domain knowledge is acquired the business analyst may start activities for the next stage or revisit work from a previous stage.
The Analyst Manager will advise if inception is sufficiently  complete to move to the next stage. The decision to move to closedown is considered a gateway for the project executive or project manager and needs to be communicated to the Analyst Manager.
