Heading Content
Product Name
Scenario Artefact (Also known as a ‘Storyboards’)
Description
Scenarios describe the steps, events, and/or actions which occur during an interaction. Usage scenarios can be very detailed, indicating exactly how someone works with the user interface, or reasonably high-level describing a critical business action, but not indicating how they are performed
Status
The development status of Scenario Artefacts for analysis models is ‘optional’ - where developed Scenario Artefacts are considered ‘formal artefacts’ for publication or review / approval purposes.
Purpose
The purpose of Scenario Artefacts are to describe a real-world example of how one or more people or organizations interact with a system. This provides a user focussed view of the system
Audience & Use
Internal:
•  Analyst: to aid understanding of the domain and allow for creation of Stories (both Epic and User)
•  Project Team: used to verify against other artefacts; and to meet stated Purpose for this Artefact
•  Test Team: to inform test cases.
External:
•  Users: to verify for accuracy, completeness and consistency from a user’s perspective
•  Suppliers: to use as input into development work
Format & Content
The methodologies allow Scenario Artefacts to be created in whatever format best suits the presentation of their content. They are typically sourced either from pre-existing documents within the project / domain, or developed by analysts in consultation with domain experts as a part of the analysis process. Scenario Artefacts are developed during the Discovery stage of an analysis project. Whilst they may start their development as discrete external documents, Scenario should, where possible, be embedded within the model (e.g. as EA document element). It is recommended that they are placed in the sub-package named: ‘Scenarios’ within the Discovery and Planning Stage package (in the Analysis Methodology Rapid top-level Package). They may also be placed in other suitable package locations at the model authors discretion. Scenario Artefacts imported into the model should also have hyperlinks to them placed within the ‘Model Management & Documents’ area, on the Navigation Page Artefact (or upon a suitable peer product diagram).
- Their development typically continues iteratively throughout the life-cycle of the analysis project.
•  Scenarios are not constructed to a formal modelling standard or Template within the Methodology; they are usually created externally by domain experts as MS Word documents
•  Whilst developed and able to stand as discrete artefacts they should always be embedded within the Analysis Model using the approved CASE Toolset (Enterprise Architect [EA])
•  Scenarios should be considered during the Discovery Stage
•  They are held within the Related Documents sub-Package, within the top-level Navigation Package, and should be accessible from the Related Documents section of the Navigation Page Artefact
Construction Guidelines
•  Text based, usually in MS Word format.
•  Development may continue iteratively throughout the lifecycle of a domain analysis project.
