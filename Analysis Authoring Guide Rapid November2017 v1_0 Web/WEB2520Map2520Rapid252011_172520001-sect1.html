Section
Content
Introduction (the opening section)
This includes a brief introduction to the Agile Approach as well as guidance on when to follow the Rapid or Generic Analysis Methodology and Authoring Guide content, as well as Document Control information — in the following sub-sections: Data Products Analysis Methodologies (Introduction) / Rapid Methodology / Generic Methodology / The Agile Approach and Principles (Introduction).
Publication Control and Management
Lists details of the Publication Control mechanisms, content review and approvals as well as outlining the changes made to the publication over time — in the following sub-sections: Revision Management / Reviews / Approvals / Using this Publication.
Analysis Methodology (Rapid)
This outlines the development and management of analysis projects and the artefacts they deliver in accordance with the Rapid Methodology — as follows:  
 
Rapid Methodology Overview (Grid):
Describes the overall approach (who, what, why, when and how). 
 
Project Controls:
Includes sections for governance, roles, project stages and product route map.
 
Business Analysis:
Covers activities that are primarily responsibility of the business analyst. 
 
Ceremonies and Processes:
Describes how Data Products works with an agile type project.
Modelling and Project Concepts
This covers the industry standard view of key modelling and other concepts / approaches used in the guide.
 
Notations
These cover the notations used within artefacts and modelling concepts. The content covers both modelling core notations (the industry standards) and Data Products Adopted Notations for developing artefacts. The adopted notations include additional applied constraints as well as hints and guidance. These notations will be supported by Toolset Guidance (e.g. Enterprise Architect — NOTE: The toolset guidance content is currently under development).
- The Core Notations content is accessible via the Modelling Concepts section, and Adopted Notations via the Artefacts section.
Artefacts
This provides guidance to the Business Analyst on the format of key artefacts and templates that they may produce during a project; as definition sections and Product Descriptions.
Specialist Software Toolset Guidance
This will cover the specialist analysis tooling used by Data Products (including Enterprise Architect).  It will NOT cover standard MS Office or ICT tooling guidance.
NOTE: The toolset guidance content is not included in this version as it is currently under development.

Audience
Audience
This guide is designed for use by Business Analysts (Model Authors) to effectively complete Analysis and other Modelling tasks; leading to the generation of a set of artefacts / deliverables for an Analysis Project.
It may also be used by other Stakeholders to understand the types of business analysis under completion.

Purpose
Purpose
The purpose of this guide is to support a fuller understanding of the analysis concepts, processes and products that may be utilised by an Analysis Project.
- It should be noted that it is not designed to be an exhaustive reference tool for all aspects of the methodologies, modelling approaches, model or artefact development, but aims to provide sufficient detail to support effective application of the core principles. Also, it does not seek to cover the wider software project management & development frameworks used within NHS Digital.
It outlines the following key aspects:
•  The main modelling approaches as well as their core notations.
•  The approved methodologies, their goals and processes, including.
–  The formal project controls (e.g. governance and management) and development processes.
–  Reviews and approvals processes.
–  General model creation and development and publication guidelines.
•  The methodology-defined set of approved artefacts, along with their adopted notations and product descriptions, including:
–  Explaining the underlying purpose and expected use of these artefacts.
–  Identifying the development context for each artefact, as well as its place in the overall project lifecycle.
•  The approved range of toolsets:
–  In particular, the currently approved CASE Toolset - Enterprise Architect [EA]).
