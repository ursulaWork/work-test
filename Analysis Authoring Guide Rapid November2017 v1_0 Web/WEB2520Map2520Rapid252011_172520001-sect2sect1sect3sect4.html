Sprint Development commences with a daily "stand up" meeting.
Who
Core team lead by Scrum Master.
Optional: Stakeholders.
Why (purpose)
A Stand-up is designed to quickly inform everyone of what's going on across the team. It's not a detailed status meeting.
The purpose is to ensure that progress and impediments can be tracked, managed, and mitigated where necessary.
When
Once a day normally during Sprints. It is best held in the morning.
Duration
15 minutes.
What
The Stand-Up is a developer focussed meetings.
During the Daily Stand-Up each team member answer the following questions:
•  What did I do yesterday?
•  What will I work on today?
•  Are there any impediment to my work?
All project members are invited to these meetings, however only the Core Team should speak (e.g. Product Owner, Business Analyst, Developer, Tester and Scrum Master).
The Daily Stand-Up meeting is not used as a problem-solving or issue resolution meeting. Issues that are raised are taken offline and usually dealt with by the relevant subgroup immediately after the meeting.
Outputs
Agile Framework
SCRUM.
Kanban.
Hint: The Scrum approach to Stand-Up meetings also benefits from using a Kanban style board.
