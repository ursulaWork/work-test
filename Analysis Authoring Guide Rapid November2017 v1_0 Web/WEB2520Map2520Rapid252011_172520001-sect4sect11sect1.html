This section outlines the Adopted Notations for the Stakeholder Business Model Artefact (SBM) — (for examples see the figures: stakeholder business model artefact — example as an activity diagram AND stakeholder business model artefact — example as a sequence diagram):
•  The Stakeholder Business Model’s format is defined as ‘free-form’ and is therefore not formally constrained for either construction or notation:
–  Where suitable business model material pre-exists for a project, sourced from the domain (e.g. as a process flow diagram) and they meet analysis requirements, they can be utilised by analysis models, and can either be referenced by or embedded into it.
–  Where no suitable pre-existing stakeholder business model is available, it may be produced as part of the analysis model - in this instance the SBM Artefact is typically developed as either a UML Activity Diagram or UML Sequence Diagram.
•  The SBM Artefact is typically sourced / developed during the Planning & Discovery stage of an analysis project.
•  At this time only very limited Adopted Notations are outlined here; however model authors may also utilise the Adopted Notations for Activity Diagram Artefacts, and Adopted Notations for Interaction Unit Artefacts, as well as the Core Notations for UML Activity Diagrams or the Core Notations for UML Sequence Diagrams to support their development.
