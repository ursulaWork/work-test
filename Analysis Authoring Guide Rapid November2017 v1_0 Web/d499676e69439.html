Stakeholder Business Model (SBM)

Description
After p in entry
A Stakeholder Business Model (SBM) is an free-form artefact. It is developed in direct cooperation with domain experts and stakeholders to identifying its main processes and interactions. SBMs may utilise any format that is easily understood by the domain and can be sourced either from preexisting content developed within it (e.g. a process-flow diagram), or may be developed by analysts as either a UML Activity Diagram or UML Sequence Diagram

Purpose
After p in entry
The purpose(s) of the Stakeholder Business Model is to:
• 
Help those developing and using the Domain Analysis Model to gain a high-level understanding of processes, information flows, participants, concepts
• 
Provide a basis upon which to ensure stakeholder engagement
• 
Support project Scoping activities to:
◦ 
Clarify the scope of the analysis
◦ 
Agree the outline business processes for domain analysis
◦ 
Support project planning
• 
Inform the development of downstream dynamic analysis artefacts (e.g. Aid the identification of candidate use cases)
• 
Support traceability between the analysis artefacts and the domain(s)

Format & Content
After p in entry
The Stakeholder Business Model is a ‘free-form’ artefact that may be developed in whatever form best meets the needs of the project (or it may use an existing domain-sourced artefact). It may also make use of pre-existing domain-developed Business Models or Artefacts. Where developed as part of the Analysis Model it may use a UML Activity Diagram or a UML Sequence Diagram and associated elements within the package structure. SBM Artefacts defined using a UML Activity Diagram represent Activity elements and the Flows between them; SBM Artefacts defined using a UML Sequence Diagram represent Object / Actor elements and the Interactions between them.
SBM Artefacts are developed during the ‘Inception’ Stage of a Project.
Where developed within the model structure the SBM Artefact should be held as follows: in a package named ‘Stakeholder Business Model’ within the ‘Rapid Analysis Methodology’ package — if the SBM was developed externally the artefact should (if possible) be embedded within the Model and a hyperlink to it placed upon a relevant area of the model’s Navigation Page.
This artefact is created using the currently approved CASE toolset (Enterprise Architect) — (unless it is an already existing domain-sourced artefact).
