“A Sprint Retrospective is a facilitated meeting in which the team reflect on a just-concluded sprint to determine what could be changed to make future sprints more productive.”
Who
Core team.
Optional: involved Stakeholders.
Why (purpose)
The purpose of the Sprint Retrospective is to help the team understand what worked well–and what didn't.
In order to promote a culture of continuous improvement.
When
At the end of each Sprint or at project closedown.
Duration
60 minutes.
What
Agile is about getting rapid feedback to make the product and development culture better.
Use Retrospectives to find out what's working so the team can continue to focus on those areas. Also, find out what's not working and use the time to find creative solutions and develop an action plan.
Every team member should have the opportunity to say:
•  What went well.
•  What is not working.
Outputs
Notes on how to improve the process.
For Data Products this is suggestions for improvement to the Rapid Authoring Guide.
Agile Framework
Scrum and Kanban.
•  For Kanban reviews, should be aligned with team milestones rather than on a fixed cadence.
