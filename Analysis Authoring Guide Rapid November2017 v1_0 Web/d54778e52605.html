Requirements Characteristics

A requirement needs to meet several criteria to be considered a "good" requirement.  The following is the a list of requirement characteristics:
• 
Cohesive: the Requirement addresses one and only one thing. 
• 
Complete: the Requirement is fully stated with no missing information. 
• 
Unique: the Requirement is not (in whole or part) repeated in other requirements or referenced artefacts. 
• 
Consistent: the Requirement does not contradict any other requirement or referenced artefact. 
• 
Correct: the Requirement meets all or part of a need identified during analysis. 
• 
Feasible: the Requirement can be implemented within the constraints of the project (even if this is identified as a future phase Requirement). 
• 
Unambiguous: the Requirement is concisely stated in language recognisable to stakeholders. It is subject to only one interpretation.
• 
Recognisable: the Requirement content should be recognisable to business stakeholders.
• 
Verifiable: the implementation of the Requirement can be verified by inspection, analysis, demonstration, or test. 
• 
Persists: the Requirement should be usable within later project-cycle activities such as design.

Wording of Requirementsspacer text
The wording used for Requirements should be clear and open to only one interpretation; Adjectives and adverbs are to be avoided. Compound statements are also prohibited.  
The wording of Requirements must adhere to the ‘MoSCoW’ — RFC2119 Standard prioritisation method.
