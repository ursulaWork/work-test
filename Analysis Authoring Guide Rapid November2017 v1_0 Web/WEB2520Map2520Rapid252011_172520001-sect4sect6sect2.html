Heading Details
Product Name/s
Story Map Artefact
Description
A ‘Story Map ’ is a Story Artefact.
 Story Maps can focus on:
•  The entire product backlog,
•  Themes within the backlog or development,
•  The sprint level, to prepare for development.
A Story Map is a diagram that shows aspects of the analysis model elements that define the functionality of the system. The diagram primarily focuses on User Story elements but can also include other elements from the analysis model such as requirements and use cases.
Status
The development status of the Story Map Artefact is ‘Optional’.
Purpose
The purpose of the Story Map Artefact is to:
•  Provide a diagrammatic representation of a projects elements, requirements, stories and use cases.
•  It may show the relationships between the elements.
•  Support sprint planning activities allowing the core team to agree the scope of a sprint and the production of a report based on the content of the diagram.
•  Support understanding the proposed scope of product as well as identifying holes and omissions in the backlog.
Audience & Use
Internal:
•  Product Owner: To understand system functionality and identify gaps.
•  Analyst: To understand system functionality, identify gaps and plan releases.
•  Project Team: To allow stories to be organised based on necessity.
•  Developers: For guidance on estimating development time.
External:
•  Domain Experts: To verify against other artefacts.
Format & Content
Story Maps are diagrams created during the Discovery stage and developed iteratively during delivery.
They are held within the following model structure package/s:
•  In the User Story Catalogue package.
•  Story Maps for Sprints can also be in individual Sprint Packages within the User Story Catalogue package.
They are created using the currently approved CASE Toolset.
Construction Guidelines
•  For details of how to construct a Story Map artefact, see the section: Story Map Adopted Notations.
