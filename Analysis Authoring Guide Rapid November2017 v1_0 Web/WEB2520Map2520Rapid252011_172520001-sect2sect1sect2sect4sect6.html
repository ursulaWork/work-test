The purpose of Stakeholder Analysis is to:
1. Provide a means of categorising Stakeholders in order to identify the most appropriate Stakeholder management approach.
2. Understand the needs of Stakeholders from their perspective; in the context of the business and project objectives.
3. Understand which Stakeholder groups / Personas should be considered as ‘Actors’ within the analysis model.
Personas
“‘Personas’ are fictional characters created as part of a Stakeholder Analysis process to represent different user types or views. They are a way of representing a Stakeholder or set of Stakeholders.”
Actor Roles
“An Actor is a participant in a process / function. Actors may represent either human or system roles that interact with other modelled entities. They are represented according to the specific core notations for each diagram type that utilises actor roles (e.g. Actor elements on Use Case Diagrams or as Swimlanes on Activity Diagrams).”
The section ‘Understanding and Identifying Actors’ provides further details to support the development of Actor roles within analysis models.
