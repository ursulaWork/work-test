“UML Sequence Diagrams represent the transmission of interactions (e.g. information such as messages or other forms of communication) sent and received between human and / or system roles, as well as defining the context and behaviours for these Interactions. A Sequence Diagram also represents the order in which interactions may occur.”
example sequence diagram
An example of a UML Sequence Diagram
The purpose of a UML Sequence Diagram is show a structured representation of behavior as a series of sequential steps over time.
It can be used to:
•  Depict workflow (e.g. message passing and how elements in general cooperate over time to achieve a result).
•  Describe the transmission and receipt of interactions (e.g. information exchanged via a conversation, document or electronic information flow) between human and / or system entities.
•  Describe the flow of information and responsibility throughout the system.
•  Create explanatory models for Scenarios, User Stories and Use Cases; the Sequence Diagram illustrates the sequence of steps undertaken.
UML Sequence Diagrams are maintained in a named model package, which contains:
•  Lifeline Object Elements (as Object or Actor Lifelines) — individual participants in an interaction, that define the roles sending or receiving interactions. ◦
–  These are represented as vertically descending lines which denote the start / end point for interactions between Lifeline Objects. They also indicate the notional order in which interactions may take place (e.g. when read from left to right and top down)
•  Sequence Diagrams are also further elaborated using:
–  Interactions — the horizontal connectors between vertical Lifeline Objects, denoting a ‘transmission’ (e.g. message) between the two Lifeline elements.
–  Fragment Regions — to enclose a discrete set of interactions
The construction of UML Sequence Diagrams are defined by their Core Notations.
