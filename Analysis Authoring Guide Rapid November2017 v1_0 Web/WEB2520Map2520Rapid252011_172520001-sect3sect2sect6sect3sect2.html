“A Stereotype is a UML Extensibility Mechanism that can be applied to an Entity to further support its definition or context (e.g. as an additional description).”
There are no example figures here; however they are illustrated for each of the defined applications).
The purpose of a Stereotype is to further define model entities; usually to specify the role they are playing within the model, for additional information or to relate them to a defined business subset.
Stereotypes may be applied to Model Packages, Diagrams, Elements and Connectors, as well as to other model entities, and typically take the form of ‘<<Stereotype description>>’.
The construction of UML Stereotypes are defined by their Core Notations.
