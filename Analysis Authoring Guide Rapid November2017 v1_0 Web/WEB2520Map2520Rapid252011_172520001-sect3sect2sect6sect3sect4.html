“Multiplicities (or cardinalities) denote how many of an entity must or may exist in relation to another entity (e.g. ‘one to many’ / ‘many to many’ or ‘n to n’). From the information shown, the options for the number of entities that must / may exist can be deduced (e.g. 1..* denotes there must always be one of the first object, and at least one or many of the second object)”
- The terms ‘Multiplicity’ and ‘Cardinality’ are used interchangeably in this publication (unless otherwise specifically stated).
Example figures showing the application of Multiplicities / Cardinalities are available within the content defining each of its uses (via the links provided).
The purpose of a Multiplicity is to specify how many of an entity may / must exist for a given processing situation.
Multiplicities may be applied in the following modelling contexts:
Diagram Type
Multiplicity application
UML Classes / Class Diagrams
Multiplicities represented on Associations
Attribute Multiplicities
The construction / use of UML Multiplicities and Cardinalities are outlined in their Core Notations; they may also be further defined by the content for each diagram-type that utilises them.
