“Agile’ is an umbrella term used to describe a general approach to software development. Though there are many flavours of Agile e.g. eXtreme Programming (XP), Scrum, Kanban, Lean Software Development – all Agile processes promote responsiveness in the forms of adaptive planning, evolutionary development, early delivery, in-line testing and bug-resolution as well as continuous improvement.‘”
The Agile approach reduces the need for projects to have heavy comprehensive planning, development and testing stages focusing on a single large release. Agile leads to cross-functional teams working on relatively small work items from a Backlog which are delivered over fixed / short periods and released frequently. The aim is to make responsiveness of requirements, delivery and feedback an inherent part of the approach.
All Agile flavours trace their approach back to the Agile Manifesto, adhering to the corresponding Agile Manifesto Principles.

The Agile approach adopted by Data Products
The Data Products Rapid Methodology is based on practice derived from the Generic Methodology and takes the "Scrum" approach to Agile. Its intention is to guide the Business Analyst around techniques and artefacts to use, while adhering to the principles of Agile (further details of this approach are outlined in the sections: Agile Concepts and Rapid Methodology Overview).
